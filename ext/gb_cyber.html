Cyberbullying

“They Hide Behind Screens Because They Can’t Face You.”

Cyberbullying is the digital version of a hit-and-run. It’s fake accounts, cruel messages, public humiliation, and constant stalking — all from behind a screen. The internet gives cowards a megaphone and a mask. They say things online they’d never dare to say to your face.

It’s not “just online.” It’s real. It hurts. And it spreads. But it’s also trackable, exposable, and—most importantly—temporary.

What cyberbullies fear the most? You not reacting. You thriving anyway. You turning the spotlight back on them, calmly and powerfully.

Why Do They Do It?

  • False sense of power
    Anonymity makes them bold. But behind that username? Still insecure. Still weak.
  • They want a reaction
    Fear, sadness, anger — any sign that they got to you. Don’t give them the satisfaction.
  • Herd mentality
    Online mobs form fast. Most don’t even know you — they just want something to attack.

What Can You Do?

  • Block, mute, report – every time. You don’t have to debate with cowards.
  • Screenshot and document – receipts speak louder than rumors.
  • Tell someone you trust – silence protects the bully, not you.
  • Take digital breaks – protect your peace, reclaim your mind.

Final Thought
Cyberbullies are loud online but empty in real life. Your worth isn’t defined by likes, comments, or cruel DMs. You don’t need to delete yourself — you need to delete their access.
